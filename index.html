Ecological Consequences of Deforestation

Deforestation has a severe impact on the environment, from destroying ecosystems to threatening biodiversity, Deforestation has a very bad impact on all living organisms The removal of trees destroys habitats, leading to disturbance and then extinction for many plants and animals. This not only has an affect not only on the living organisms but also on the water cycle thus reducing rainfall inducing drought Soil erosion is another thing that is caused by Deforestation. Additionally, Deforestation is a major contributer to climate change.The loss of forests not only weakens the planets ability to regulate temperatures but also weakens the planets ability to maintain ecological balance, thus making conservation efforts neccessary.
